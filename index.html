Always replace the crush washer
Toyota drain plugs use a copper or aluminum crush washer. Replace it every oil change — they cost $1–2 at any Toyota dealer and prevent slow leaks.
Hand-tighten first
Thread by hand to avoid cross-threading. Only use the torque wrench once the plug seats cleanly by hand.
6-point sockets only
12-point sockets grab corners, not flats. On a 14mm drain plug, that's a one-way ticket to a rounded head.

From the Shop Floor

PRO TIPS

01
Warm engine = faster drain
Let the car idle 2 minutes before draining. Warm oil flows out 3× faster than cold. Don't let it sit — hot oil burns.
02
67mm first, every time
On an unknown Toyota, try the 67mm filter socket before the 64mm. It fits 80% of models. Saves a socket swap.
03
No power tools on the plug
Impact gun to remove is fine. Never use power tools to reinstall — always finish with a torque wrench. Impact = stripped threads.
04
Label your tray
Add a card inside the tray with sizes, torque spec, and crush washer part number. Your future self will thank you at 6am.
